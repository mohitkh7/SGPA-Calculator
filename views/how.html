How SGPA is Calculated ?
The Semester Grade Point Average (SGPA) and Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) is calculated as weighted average of valid and virtual credit points secured by the students.

A SGPA is a measure of performance of the student in a semester. It is the ration of total credit points secured by student in various courses registered in a semester and the total course credits taken during semester.

For calculation of SGPA, Grades are converted to their Grade Points according to this table.

Letter Grade Grade Points Description Range of Marks (%)
O 10 Excellent 90-100
A+ 9 Excellent 80-89
A 8 Very Good 70-79
B+ 7 Good 60-69
B 6 Above Average 50-59
C 5 Average 40-49
P 4 Pass 35-39
F 0 Fail 0-34
Ab 0 Absent Absent

Each grade point is then multiplied by the course credit score of that subject.
For Example - If you scored A+ grade in Applied Mathematics then it is converted to grade points (i.e. 9) which will be multipled with credit score of subject (i.e. 4)

After taking summation of all credit points it is divided by total credits in the semester (i.e. 30) which gives the SGPA.
